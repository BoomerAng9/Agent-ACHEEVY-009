## 2. Layered Search Architecture (The Engine)

To maintain load balancing and handle spikes in requests, Per|Form utilizes a constructed, layered approach to data retrieval and generation:

### 2.1 The Three Tiers of Search
1. **Quick Search (Tier 1)**: Powered by Gemini 3.1 Pro (High/Low) and Gemini 3 Flash. Used for rapid, superficial queries, placeholder searches, and immediate responses. We "think for the user" by having pre-computed/cached answers ready.
2. **Deep Research (Tier 2)**: Powered by DeepMind's deep research capabilities via OpenRouter (Gemini 3.0 Pro / 3.1 Pro). Used for historical data compilation and complex analysis.
3. **Targeted Crawl (Tier 3)**: Powered by the Brave API. Reserved specifically for when the deep research engine identifies a targeted need to index localized news, specific interviews, or recent media clippings.

### 2.2 System Resilience
The orchestrator load-balances these requests. If traffic spikes, Tier 1 is prioritized. Tier 2 and Tier 3 are queued for heavy analytic processes.

## 10. Core Modalities: State Analytics & Transfer Matchmaker

### 10.1 High School State-by-State Analytics
We do not rely solely on mainstream publications (which miss hidden gems like a "0-star RB Cam Newton from Texas").
- **Criteria**: We index the top 50-100 high school players **per state**.
- **Metric**: Based PURELY on previous year stat production, not star ratings.
- **Deep Research**: Local media, local news, and community rumblings are scraped.
- **User Submissions**: A submission portal allows users to submit "players to look out for" which are then queued for Deep Research.

### 10.2 NIL & Transfer Portal Alignment
- **NIL Valuation**: The player's AGI grade directly determines their perceived NIL value in our ecosystem.
- **Transfer Portal**: Using the AGI grades, we algorithmically align transfer portal players with teams that have exact roster openings matching their position and skill archetype.
